⚙️ 7-Day CAD Learning Journey

A day-by-day showcase of my progress in Computer-Aided Design.

Day 1: The First Sketch

Topic: Introduction to the CAD interface, navigation, and fundamental 2D sketching tools.

My Learning: The journey began with familiarizing myself with the software's workspace. I learned to create simple 2D shapes using basic tools like **lines, circles, and arcs**. The key takeaway was understanding the concept of a **fully defined sketch**, where every line and point is locked in place with dimensions and constraints.

Day 2: Advanced Sketching & Constraints

Topic: Mastering geometric constraints and using advanced sketching tools for complex profiles.

My Learning: This day focused on precision and efficiency. I explored tools like **patterns, mirroring, and splines**. The main focus was applying **geometric constraints** (e.g., tangent, concentric, parallel) to ensure my designs were robust and easily editable for future modifications.

Day 3: Bringing Ideas to Life in 3D

Topic: Basic 3D Part Modeling using extrude and revolve features.

My Learning: The most exciting part—turning 2D sketches into 3D objects! I learned to use the **Extrude** command to give sketches depth and the **Revolve** command to create cylindrical or spherical parts. This marked a huge step from flat drawings to solid models.

Day 4: Advanced 3D Modeling

Topic: Creating complex geometry with sweeps, lofts, fillets, and chamfers.

My Learning: I moved beyond simple blocks and cylinders. I used the **Sweep** feature to create parts along a path (like a pipe) and the **Loft** feature to create smooth transitions between different shapes. I also learned to add finishing touches like **Fillets** (rounded edges) and **Chamfers** (angled edges).

Day 5: The Art of Assembly

Topic: Assembling multiple parts and defining their mechanical relationships with mates.

My Learning: I learned how to create complex systems by bringing individual parts together. The key was defining **Mates** to control how parts fit and move in relation to one another. I built a small, functional assembly, ensuring all components interacted correctly as they would in the real world.

Day 6: Technical Drawings & Annotations

Topic: Creating 2D manufacturing drawings from 3D models.

My Learning: I learned how to translate a 3D model into a standardized 2D technical drawing. This involved creating different views (front, top, side), adding precise **dimensions**, and including important **annotations** to provide all the information needed for manufacturing.

Day 7: Rendering & Project Showcase

Topic: Creating photorealistic renderings and presenting the final project.

My Learning: The final day was about presentation. I used the software's **rendering** tools to apply materials, lighting, and environments to my assembly, creating a stunning, photorealistic image. This showcased the final design in a visually appealing way, ready for a portfolio.
